Step Deck Trucking: The Height Advantage in Open Deck Freight

The step deck trailer — also called a drop deck — gives you a critical advantage over standard flatbed operators: the ability to haul freight that's too tall for a regular flatbed without always requiring an oversize permit. The lower rear deck sits approximately 3.5 feet off the ground (vs. 5 feet for flatbed), allowing you to legally haul freight up to 10 feet tall on the lower section.

What loads can you expect? Step deck freight includes construction equipment, agricultural machinery, modular building sections, large HVAC units, manufacturing equipment, and any cargo too tall for a standard flatbed but lighter than what requires a lowboy. The construction, agriculture, and industrial equipment sectors are your primary markets, with strong demand across the Sun Belt and Midwest.

Maximizing your two-deck advantage: The step deck's two-level configuration allows creative loading strategies. Shorter freight can go on the upper deck while taller items ride the lower deck. This flexibility lets you haul combination loads that maximize your payload value. First Bridge dispatchers understand step deck loading strategies and find loads that take full advantage of your equipment.

Permits and oversized loads: While the step deck reduces permit needs compared to flatbed, oversized loads that exceed 8'6" width or 10' height still require state permits and sometimes pilot cars. First Bridge handles all permit coordination and ensures all accessorials are properly compensated before you accept any load.
