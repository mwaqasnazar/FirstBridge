Why Fuel Price Comparison Matters for Truck Drivers

Diesel fuel is the single largest operating expense for most owner operators, typically accounting for 30-40% of gross revenue. Even a difference of just $0.10 per gallon adds up fast. On a 1,000-mile trip in a truck averaging 6.5 MPG, you will use approximately 154 gallons of diesel. At $0.10 per gallon savings, that is $15.40 saved per trip -- over $8,000 per year at 120,000 annual miles.

Strategic fuel stop planning along your route, not just convenient stops, is one of the easiest ways to improve your bottom line without driving a single extra mile.

Top Tips for Reducing Fuel Costs

  • Plan fuel stops before leaving: Identify the cheapest diesel along your planned route using apps like GasBuddy, Trucker Path, or this tool
  • Use fuel discount programs: TCS Fuel, EFS, Comdata, and Pilot/Flying J Pro accounts offer significant per-gallon discounts
  • Buy fuel in tax-advantaged states: States like Texas, Oklahoma, and Mississippi have lower diesel fuel taxes than states like California or New York
  • Maintain proper tire pressure: Under-inflated tires can reduce fuel economy by 2-3%
  • Reduce idle time: Each hour of idling burns roughly 0.8 gallons of diesel -- that adds up to hundreds of dollars monthly
  • Use cruise control on highways: Consistent speed significantly improves MPG versus variable acceleration
  • Reduce high-speed driving: Fuel efficiency drops sharply above 65 mph -- slowing down from 70 to 65 can save 10-15% on fuel
  • Load fuel cards before crossing state lines: High-tax states near state borders are often the most expensive fuel stops

Frequently Asked Questions

Diesel prices vary by location and day, so no single chain is always cheapest. However, Love's, Pilot Flying J, and independent truck stops often have competitive pricing. The most reliable way to find the cheapest diesel is to use fuel comparison apps like GasBuddy, Trucker Path, or your fleet card's fuel locator. Fuel discount programs through your fleet card often provide better savings than shopping by chain.

Fuel cards typically offer discounts ranging from $0.05 to $0.40 per gallon depending on the card, volume, and negotiated terms. At $0.15 per gallon average savings across 40,000 gallons per year (typical for one truck), that is $6,000 annually per truck. Major fleet card programs include TCS Fuel, Comdata, EFS (WEX), and Relay Payments.

States with the lowest diesel fuel taxes include Oklahoma, Mississippi, New Mexico, and Alaska, which typically have state diesel excise taxes under $0.25 per gallon. States like California ($0.74+), Pennsylvania, and Washington have among the highest fuel taxes. When planning routes near state borders, it is worth checking if fueling on the other side of the state line offers meaningful savings.

Beyond fuel, key amenities for truck drivers include: truck parking (especially reserved/covered spots), clean showers, laundry facilities, CAT scale access, DEF fluid availability, WiFi, restaurant or food options, and roadside assistance services. Large chains like Pilot Flying J and Love's offer mobile apps that let you reserve showers and parking in advance, saving time on the road.
