How to Start a Trucking Company

Starting a trucking company can be a profitable business opportunity for drivers who want independence and higher income. Understanding the steps, costs, and requirements is essential to building a successful operation from day one.

Steps to Start a Trucking Company

  1. 1

    Create a Business Plan

    Define your services, target equipment type, budget, and target market. Know your break-even rate before you spend a dollar.

  2. 2

    Register Your Business

    Choose LLC or Corporation structure and register with your state. Get an EIN from the IRS for your business tax ID.

  3. 3

    Obtain MC Authority

    Apply for your MC number and USDOT number through FMCSA. Allow 20-25 business days for authority activation.

  4. 4

    Get Commercial Insurance

    Secure primary liability, cargo, and physical damage insurance. FMCSA requires minimum $750,000 liability coverage.

  5. 5

    Set Up Operations

    Open a business bank account, set up factoring if needed, and get compliance documents (BOC-3, UCR) in place before hauling.

Tips for Success

✔Start with realistic financial planning — know your monthly fixed costs before accepting any load
✔Avoid low-paying loads early on — they create bad habits and cash flow problems
✔Use a professional dispatch service for load consistency while building broker relationships
✔Track every expense from day one — fuel, tolls, maintenance, insurance, and fees
✔Keep 2-3 months of operating expenses in reserve for slow periods or repairs
