Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about diesel fuel prices for truck drivers

The current U.S. national average diesel price is updated weekly on this page using data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA). Prices typically range from $3.40 to $4.50 per gallon depending on region and market conditions. Check the price banner at the top of this page for today's latest figure.

The EIA publishes updated weekly retail diesel prices every Monday for the previous week. This page pulls the latest available data from the EIA API automatically each time you visit, so you always see the most current published prices without needing to refresh manually.

Diesel prices vary across states due to several factors including state and local fuel taxes, distance from refineries and distribution hubs, regional supply and demand, environmental regulations (such as California's CARB standards), and local market competition. States like California and New York typically have higher prices, while Gulf Coast states often have lower prices due to proximity to refineries.

Owner operators can reduce fuel costs by: maintaining proper tire pressure (improves MPG by up to 3%), using fuel discount programs like TCS, EFS, or Comdata, planning fuel stops in lower-cost states, reducing idle time, driving at consistent highway speeds, and using cruise control. Our dispatchers at First Bridge also help plan routes that minimize fuel costs and deadhead miles.
