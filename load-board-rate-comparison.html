Why Comparing Freight Rates Is Critical

Many owner operators accept loads based on the total dollar amount without comparing it to current market rates. A $2,200 load might sound good until you realize the market average for that lane and equipment type is $2,700. Accepting below-market loads consistently means you are leaving thousands of dollars on the table every month.

Before accepting any broker offer, always calculate the rate per mile and compare it against current market data. This tool gives you an instant benchmark so you can negotiate from a position of knowledge.

Rate Profitability Indicators

  • Excellent (15%+ above market): Accept immediately. Build the relationship with this broker.
  • Good (5-15% above market): Solid load. Accept or push for a small increase.
  • Fair (within 5% of market): Acceptable. Consider lane positioning before deciding.
  • Below Market (5-15% under): Try to negotiate up. Know your break-even before accepting.
  • Low Rate (15%+ below market): Decline or counter aggressively. This rate likely does not cover your expenses.

Tips for Negotiating Higher Freight Rates

  • Know the market before you call: Having data on hand puts you in a stronger position
  • Counter immediately: Never accept the first offer -- brokers always have room to move
  • Mention your costs: Diesel, tolls, and maintenance make low rates unsustainable
  • Use lane data: "DAT shows $2.55 average on this lane" is hard to argue with
  • Be willing to walk away: Brokers respect carriers who know their value
  • Build relationships: Brokers pay more to carriers they trust and rely on
  • Use a professional dispatcher: Dispatchers negotiate full-time and consistently secure 15-25% above market
