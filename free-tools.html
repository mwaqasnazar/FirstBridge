Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from owner operators about trucking rates, costs, and profitability

Average trucking rates in the United States typically range from $2.00 to $3.50 per mile depending on freight type, fuel prices, lane demand, and current market conditions. Specialized freight like flatbed or reefer often commands higher rates than dry van. Use our Rate Per Mile Calculator to evaluate any load instantly.

Owner operators calculate profit by subtracting all operating expenses from total load revenue. Key expenses include fuel, tolls, maintenance reserves, dispatch fees, insurance, and permits. What remains after all deductions is your net profit. A healthy load should return a profit margin of 15-20% or higher. Try our Load Profit Calculator to analyze any load before accepting it.

Cost per mile is the total of all your operating expenses divided by the miles you drive. It includes fuel, maintenance, truck loan payments, insurance, permits, and other fixed and variable costs. The industry average for owner operators runs between $1.50 and $1.80 per mile. Knowing your CPM ensures you never accept a load that pays less than it costs to run. Use our Cost Per Mile Calculator to find your exact number.

Fuel is consistently one of the largest expenses in trucking, typically representing 30-40% of total operating costs. Calculating fuel cost before a trip lets you determine whether a load will be profitable, helps you negotiate better rates with brokers, and prevents you from accepting loads that look good on the surface but lose money after fuel. Our Fuel Cost Calculator gives you an instant estimate based on your MPG and diesel price.

The break-even rate is the minimum rate per mile required to cover all your operating expenses without making a profit or a loss. Every load you accept below your break-even rate costs you money. To stay in business and grow, you need to consistently earn above your break-even rate. Experts recommend targeting at least 15-20% above your break-even to maintain a healthy margin. Calculate yours with our Break-Even Rate Calculator.
